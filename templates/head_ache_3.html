HEAD ACHE MEDICATION INFORMATION






AGE (Adults)

Age group of 18-65 Years


What To Do

For adults over the age of 18, acetaminophen (Tylenol), aspirin, ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin), and naproxen (Aleve) are commonly used for headaches.

Aspirin, acetaminophen, ibuprofen, and naproxen are commonly used for headaches in adults, but it's important to follow the recommended dosage and avoid overusing these medications, as this can lead to rebound headaches.

However, individuals with liver disease should avoid acetaminophen and those with a history of stomach ulcers or bleeding disorders should avoid aspirin.

If headaches persist or become more severe, it's important to seek medical attention to rule out any underlying conditions.



It is important to note that these medications should only be used as directed and under the guidance of a healthcare provider. Dosage and frequency of medication can vary based on age, weight, and other health factors.




POINTS TO REMEMBER


1.Types of headaches: There are different types of headaches, including tension headaches, migraines, cluster headaches, and others. Each type may have different symptoms, triggers, and treatment options.

2.Triggers: Headaches can be triggered by a variety of factors, including stress, lack of sleep, dehydration, certain foods or drinks, hormonal changes, and environmental factors. Identifying and avoiding triggers can help prevent headaches.

3.Medications: There are different types of medications that can be used to treat headaches, including over-the-counter pain relievers like acetaminophen and ibuprofen, prescription medications, and alternative therapies like acupuncture. It's important to follow the recommended dosage and avoid overusing medication to prevent rebound headaches.

4.Lifestyle changes: Making lifestyle changes like getting regular exercise, practicing stress management techniques, staying hydrated, and maintaining a healthy diet can help prevent headaches.

5.When to seek medical attention: If headaches are severe, persistent, or accompanied by other symptoms like fever, neck stiffness, or vision changes, it's important to seek medical attention to rule out any underlying conditions.
