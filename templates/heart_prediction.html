HEART DISEASE PREDICTION



1. Age: The age of the patient in years.


2. Sex: The gender of the patient (1 = male; 0 = female).


3. Chest Pain Type (cp): The type of chest pain experienced by the patient. It can take one of four values:
Value 1: typical angina
Value 2: atypical angina
Value 3: non-anginal pain
Value 4: asymptomatic


4. Resting Blood Pressure (trestbps): The resting blood pressure of the patient (in mm Hg).


5. Serum Cholesterol (chol): The serum cholesterol level of the patient (in mg/dl).


6. Fasting Blood Sugar (fbs): Whether or not the patient's fasting blood sugar is greater than 120 mg/dl (1 = true; 0 = false).


7. Resting Electrocardiographic Results (restecg): The results of the resting electrocardiogram, which can take one of three values:
Value 0: normal
Value 1: having ST-T wave abnormality (T wave inversions and/or ST elevation or depression of > 0.05 mV)


8. Maximum Heart Rate Achieved (thalach): The maximum heart rate achieved during the exercise test.


9. Exercise Induced Angina (exang): Whether or not the patient experienced exercise-induced angina (1 = yes; 0 = no).


10. Oldpeak: ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest.


11. Slope: The slope of the peak exercise ST segment, which can take one of three values:
Value 0: upsloping
Value 1: flat
Value 2: downsloping


12. Number of Major Vessels (ca): The number of major vessels (0-3) colored by fluoroscopy.


13. Thalassemia (thal): A blood disorder called thalassemia, which can take one of three values:
Value 1: normal
Value 2: fixed defect
Value 3: reversible defect
