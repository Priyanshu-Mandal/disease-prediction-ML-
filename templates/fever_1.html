FEVER MEDICATION INFORMATION


AGE (Infants and toddlers)

Age group of 0-1 Years


What To Do

Age group : 0-3 months

Temperature : 100.4 F (38 C) or Higher Taken Orally

1. Call The Doctor, Even if Your child doesn't have any other signs or symptoms.


Age group : 3-6 months

Temperature : Up to 102 F (38.9 C) Taken Orally

1. Encourage your child to rest and drink plenty of fluids.
2. Medication isn't needed.
3. Call the doctor if your child seems unusally irratable or uncomfortable.

Temperature : Above 102 F (38.9 C) Taken Orally

1. Call the Doctor; he or she may recommend that you bring your child in for an checking the health.


Age group : 6-24 months

Temperature : Above 104 F (38.9 C) Taken Orally

1. Give your child acetaminophen (Tylenol, others).
2. If your child is age 6 months or older, ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin, others) is OK, too.
3. Read the label carefully for proper dosage.
4. Don't give aspirin to an infant or toddler.
5. Call the doctor if the fever doesn't respond to the medication or lasts longer than one day.
